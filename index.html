Rahi — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 9 June 2026

Rahi ("Rahi", "we", "us") is a driving companion app that turns your phone's motion and location sensors into a personal driving score and coaching. This policy explains, in plain language, what data Rahi collects, what stays on your phone, what goes to our servers, and the choices and rights you have.

We have written this policy to be honest and specific. If anything here is unclear, contact us at the address in Section 11.

1. The one thing to know first: your video never leaves your phone

If you use Rahi's recording feature, any video is stored only on your device. Video is never uploaded to our servers, never sent to any third party, and never seen by us. You can delete the video for any trip at any time from within the app while keeping (or also deleting) the trip's score.

What we do process in the cloud is the motion and location sensor data from your drive — not images of the road, faces, or number plates.

2. What data we collect

2.1 Data processed on our servers (cloud)

When you record a trip and it uploads, the following leaves your device and is processed on our servers:

  • Location data — GPS latitude, longitude, and speed during the trip.
  • Motion sensor data — accelerometer, gyroscope, and (if your phone has one) barometric-altitude readings, with timestamps.
  • Trip metadata — start time, duration, distance, and the vehicle you selected for the trip.
  • Derived results — the driving score, detected driving events (e.g. a hard brake), road segments, and AI-generated coaching text computed from the above.
  • Crash reports (if enabled) — when the app crashes or hits an error, a diagnostic record is sent so we can fix it: the error type and a short technical stack trace, the app version, and your device model and Android version. This contains no personal data, no location, and no trip content. You can turn crash reports off in Settings → Privacy.

2.2 Data stored only on your device (local)

  • Video (if recorded) — see Section 1. Local only.

2.3 Account data

  • Rahi works anonymously by default — you can record and score trips without giving us any personal identifier.
  • If you choose to back up your account, you provide an email address and password. This lets you recover your trips after reinstalling or switching phones. Providing an email is optional.

2.4 What we do not collect

  • We do not use any third-party analytics, advertising, or crash-tracking SDKs. There are no trackers in the app. Crash reports go only to our own servers, not to any third party, and can be turned off.
  • We do not collect your contacts, photos, microphone audio for any purpose other than (optionally) recording your own local trip video, or any data unrelated to driving.

4. Where your data is stored

Cloud data (sensor data and derived results) is stored with our infrastructure provider Supabase, in a data centre located in Mumbai, India (ap-south-1). Our trip-processing service runs on a server hosted in India.

Coaching text is generated by sending an anonymised, numeric summary of the trip (scores, event counts, road types — no raw GPS trace and no personal identifiers) to a large-language-model provider. We do not send your name, email, or precise location to that provider.

5. How long we keep data

  • Raw sensor uploads (the original sensor files) are automatically deleted from cloud storage within 24 hours of processing.
  • Processed trip data (your scores, events, and coaching) is kept so you can review your history, until you delete the trip or your account.
  • Local video stays until you delete it or uninstall the app.

You can delete any individual trip, or your entire account and its data, from within the app at any time.

6. How we use your data

We use your data only to:

  1. Compute and show your driving score, trip timeline, and coaching.
  2. Let you review your driving history and trends.
  3. Maintain and improve the accuracy of the scoring and detection.

We do not sell your personal data. We do not share your individual, identifiable driving data with insurers, advertisers, or any third party.

If, in future, Rahi offers features built on aggregated, anonymised road data (for example a shared hazard map), such data is statistically combined across many drivers so that an individual trip or person cannot be identified, and any such feature will be described to you before it applies.

7. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction (including under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023), you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correct inaccurate data.
  • Delete your data (via in-app trip/account deletion, or by contacting us).
  • Withdraw consent by deleting your account and uninstalling the app.

To exercise any right you cannot complete in-app, contact us (Section 11).

8. Security

We protect your data with encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) in transit and provider-managed encryption at rest. Access to cloud data is restricted by per-user access controls so that one user cannot read another user's trips. No system is perfectly secure, but we take reasonable measures appropriate to the sensitivity of the data.

9. Children

Rahi is intended for licensed drivers and is not directed at children under 18. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18. If you believe a minor has used the app, contact us and we will delete the associated data.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above, and significant changes will be communicated in-app before they take effect.
